# ElyDoc Content Guidelines

## Tone of Voice

ElyDoc copy should be:

- calm
- professional
- warm
- clear
- concise
- clinically responsible
- evidence-based
- reassuring
- discreet
- non-judgmental

It should not be:

- hypey
- salesy
- overly casual
- patronising
- fear-based
- gimmicky
- influencer-like
- Americanised
- spammy
- overpromising

Use Irish/UK English spelling.

Examples:

Use:
- "specialist GP"
- "clinically appropriate"
- "where suitable"
- "private referral"
- "employer note"
- "work absence note"
- "online GP consultation"

Avoid:
- "primary care provider"
- "awesome"
- "game changer"
- "no brainer"
- "life-changing"
- "instant meds"
- "get prescribed today"
- "skip the doctor"
- "guaranteed"

## Brand Voice

The clinic should sound like:

- a thoughtful Irish GP
- a modern healthcare organisation
- a trusted clinical service
- an evidence-based digital clinic

Not like:

- a pharmaceutical ecommerce site
- an influencer wellness brand
- a corporate insurer
- a generic AI health startup

## Homepage Copy Principles

Homepage copy should be concise.

The homepage should explain:

- what ElyDoc is
- who it helps
- what services are offered
- why specialist GP standards matter
- when online care is appropriate
- how booking works
- what limitations exist
- how employers can enquire

Do not overload the homepage with long medical explanations.

Use dedicated service pages and health guides for depth.

## Clinical Safety Language

Use careful wording.

Preferred phrases:

- "where clinically appropriate"
- "following consultation with a GP"
- "your doctor may recommend in-person assessment"
- "not suitable for emergencies"
- "this service is designed for suitable non-emergency issues"
- "some conditions require physical examination, blood tests or imaging"
- "we will signpost you to appropriate care if online care is not suitable"

Avoid:

- "instant prescription"
- "guaranteed treatment"
- "we can treat this online"
- "avoid seeing a doctor"
- "prescription guaranteed"
- "no appointment needed"
- "no examination required"
- "safe for everyone"

## Service Descriptions

### Online GP

Position as:

"Online GP consultations for suitable non-emergency health concerns, with clear signposting where in-person assessment or investigations are required."

### Weight Management

Position as:

"GP-led weight management care, including GLP-1 medication where clinically appropriate."

Avoid making GLP-1 medication sound like the default.

Emphasise:

- medical suitability
- realistic expectations
- safety
- follow-up
- lifestyle foundations
- side effect monitoring

### Hair Loss

Position as:

"GP-led advice and management for male pattern hair loss."

Avoid:

- guaranteed regrowth
- cure language
- exaggerated before/after claims

### Employer Notes for Work Absence

Position as:

"Employer notes / work absence notes where clinically appropriate following a brief GP consultation."

Do not imply notes are automatic.

Make clear:

"ElyDoc does not currently offer Illness Benefit or social welfare certification. Illness Benefit certification generally requires completion of an IB1 form by your own GP or appropriate treating doctor."

### Prescriptions

Position as:

"Selected prescriptions where clinically appropriate, including bridging prescriptions for established medications and short-term treatment for suitable conditions."

Make clear that controlled/restricted drugs are not prescribed.

### Referrals

Position as:

"Private referrals where clinically appropriate, issued to the patient to forward to their chosen private provider."

Make clear that some referrals/investigations require in-person assessment.

## Clinical Safety & Service Limitations

This should be framed positively.

Use heading:

"Clinical Safety & Service Limitations"

Suggested intro:

"Online GP care is convenient, but it is not suitable for every medical issue. ElyDoc uses clear clinical boundaries to help ensure patients are directed to the right form of care."

## Doctor Credential Messaging

Emphasise:

- vocationally trained GPs
- Irish specialist GP training
- Irish Medical Council registration
- Specialist Division for General Practice

Suggested copy:

"Our consultations are provided by vocationally trained GPs who have completed specialist GP training in Ireland and are registered on the Specialist Division for General Practice with the Irish Medical Council."

Do not write:

"Our doctors are better than competitors."

Instead write:

"Specialist GP training is central to our clinical model."

## Employer Content

Employer content should be more B2B/professional.

Tone:

- credible
- practical
- confidential
- benefits-focused
- governance-aware

Core employer message:

"ElyDoc helps Irish employers provide convenient access to doctor-led online GP care as an employee benefit."

Emphasise:

- employee wellbeing
- timely access
- reduced unnecessary time away from work
- confidentiality
- specialist GP standards
- simple implementation
- contact for demo/pricing

Privacy wording:

"Clinical consultations remain confidential between the employee and doctor. Employers do not receive clinical details from employee consultations."

## SEO Content

Articles should be:

- medically accurate
- readable for laypeople
- Irish-specific where relevant
- structured with headings
- supported by FAQs
- internally linked to services
- reviewed by a doctor where appropriate

Avoid low-quality AI content.

Every article should answer a real patient question.

Good article style:

- clear title
- short introduction
- plain-English explanation
- safety caveats
- when to seek urgent care
- when ElyDoc may help
- FAQs
- related articles

## Final Copy Rule

The copy should increase trust, not pressure.

Patients should feel:

- informed
- reassured
- respected
- clear on next steps
- clear on limitations
